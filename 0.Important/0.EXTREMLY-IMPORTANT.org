* Extreme Cycle: 0 → 100 → 0

** 0–20 | Baseline (Stability)
- Normal physiology and cognition
- Predictable emotional range
- Low stress load
- Routine adaptation only

** 20–40 | Tension Build
- Early stress signals appear
- Increased vigilance
- Minor physiological drift
- Psychological anticipation of disruption

** 40–60 | Transition Zone
- System strain becomes noticeable
- Decision speed increases
- Energy redistribution begins
- Emotional volatility rises

** 60–80 | Escalation
- High stress load
- Narrowed perception
- Strong survival prioritization
- Physiological efficiency replaces comfort

** 80–100 | Critical Peak
- Full adaptive override
- Reflex dominance over deliberation
- Pain tolerance and dissociation possible
- Maximum output, minimum redundancy

** 100 → 80 | Collapse Release
- System fatigue becomes unavoidable
- Control starts returning from reflex to cognition
- Emotional rebound begins
- Physical depletion evident

** 80 → 60 | Stabilization Attempt
- Rebalancing of nervous system
- Sleep pressure increases
- Cognitive clarity slowly returns
- Hormonal normalization starts

** 60 → 40 | Reintegration
- Identity and narrative reconstruction
- Physical recovery accelerates
- Emotional processing active
- Decision systems reinitialize

** 40 → 20 | Re-entry to Normal Function
- Energy reserves rebuild
- Stress markers drop
- Cognitive flexibility returns
- Social and environmental re-connection

** 20 → 0 | Baseline Restoration
- Homeostasis restored
- Sensory and emotional balance normalized
- System returns to low-load adaptability
- Memory of peak state integrated or archived

* Entry Vectors (How extremes begin)
- Environmental triggers (heat, cold, scarcity, conflict)
- Social triggers (authority, group panic, isolation)
- Internal triggers (fatigue, hunger, hormonal shifts)
- Sudden vs gradual onset patterns

* Detection / Early Warning Systems
- Signal recognition before escalation
- Body-state monitoring (breath, pulse, tension)
- Cognitive drift indicators (narrowing, impulsivity)
- Emotional pre-collapse markers

* Control Interfaces (Levers of influence)
- Breath as primary regulator
- Attention steering (what is filtered in/out)
- Movement regulation (pace, posture, force)
- Language / self-talk structure
- Micro-decision gating (delay vs execute)

* Failure Modes
- Collapse (system shutdown)
- Fragmentation (loss of coherence)
- Overreaction loops (feedback amplification)
- Dissociation / detachment loss
- Recovery blockage

* Recovery Architecture (post-cycle rebuilding system)
- Sleep architecture control
- Memory integration vs suppression
- Physical repair prioritization
- Psychological meaning reconstruction
- Gradual stress re-exposure

* Adaptation / Learning Layer
- Pattern extraction from events
- Updating thresholds of tolerance
- Rewriting reflex responses
- Building future resistance profiles
