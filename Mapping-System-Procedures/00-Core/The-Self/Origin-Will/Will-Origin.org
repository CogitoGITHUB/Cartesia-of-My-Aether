* Will Properties

** Autonomy
Will flows from internal determination, not external compulsion.
Research: [[https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/freewill/][Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy - Free Will]]

** Direction
Will is directed toward something - a goal, object, or state.
Research: Bratman (1987) on intention as committed desire

** Persistence
Will persists over time despite obstacles.
Research: [[https://www.sciencenewstoday.org/is-free-will-an-illusion-what-psychology-and-neuroscience-suggest][Science News Today - Free Will Research]]

* Research Notes

** Libet's Research (1983)
Benjamin Libet found "readiness potential" precedes conscious will by ~350ms, suggesting unconscious origins of willing action. This does not negate will but suggests the will is shaped by pre-conscious processes.

** Compatibilism
Many philosophers argue free will is compatible with determinism - that freedom consists not in absence of causation but in acting according to one's own desires and reasoning (Hume, Dennett).
