* The Unchanging Axis

The Self is the unchanging axis in a universe of constant change. Research on stillness and silence validates this foundational practice.

* The Neuroscience of Stillness

** Silence and Brain Connectivity
Research shows that silent meditation practice reduces the connection between:
- Language network
- Dorsal attention network (DAN)
- Default mode network (DMN)

This "decoupling" creates the experience of stillness - reduced thoughts and deeper meditative states.

Research: [[https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s12671-024-02316-7][Silence Practice Modulates Brain Connectivity - Springer]]

** Mental Silence
In Sahaja yoga meditation, experienced meditators show:
- Initial intense neural self-control to silence the mind
- Then progressively reduced activation as silence deepens
- Final state localized to right inferior frontal cortex

This demonstrates that stillness is trainable and has measurable neural correlates.

Research: [[https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25671603/][Monitoring Neural Activity of Mental Silence - PubMed]]

- Cognitive-affective flexibility
- Reflective wisdom
- Prosocial behavior
