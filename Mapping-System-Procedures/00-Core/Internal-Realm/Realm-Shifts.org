* Internal Shifts Mapping

** What Shifts
- Beliefs about self and world
- Emotional states
- Philosophical positions
- Value hierarchies

** What Remains (The Self)
- Observer at the center
- Will origin
- Consciousness root

* Research on Self-Observation

** Detached Self-Observation
Recent research frames the observing self as a "developmentally important competence" that can be cultivated through practice.

Research: [[https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2025.1659774/full][Frontiers - Detached Self-Observation as Developmental Catalyst]]

** Cognitive-Affective Flexibility
Studies show self-observation fosters:
- Cognitive-afflective flexibility
- Reflective wisdom
- Prosocial behavior
- Reduced automaticity

* Mapping Protocol

1. Track what shifts (symptoms)
2. Identify what remains (root)
3. Map the relationship
4. Document the pattern
5. Return to center
